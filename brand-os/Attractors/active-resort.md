## Active Resort

Energy without extremity — the brand celebrates the body in motion within a premium, warm environment. This is not survival gear or peak-performance obsession. It is the joy of movement integrated into a resort lifestyle: morning run along the beach, yoga on the terrace, recovery in the spa, evening with the community. Performance and pleasure are the same thing.

## Visual code
- **Typography:** Warm geometric-humanist sans-serif — TT Commons, Circular, Inter with personality. Medium weight. Lowercase in informal contexts. Short punchy layouts with movement as compositional element.
- **Palette:** Sand, warm terracotta, sage green, coral accent, bright natural light. Mediterranean or coastal warmth. High natural saturation, never neon. The palette of bodies outdoors in good weather.
- **Geometry:** Slightly rounded — sport-aerodynamic without being aggressive. Open layouts suggesting expansive outdoor space. Horizon lines as structural element.
- **Density:** Airy — sea, sky, or mountain occupies 60% of frame. Human figures in motion in natural settings. Movement blur acceptable as energy signal.
- **Textures:** Salt water, warm sand, mountain grass, timber decking, natural-weave textiles. Surfaces that invite barefoot contact and physical comfort.

## Verbal code
- **Tone:** Warm, energizing, genuinely enthusiastic. Speaks like a trainer who loves what they do, not like a corporate wellness brand. Personal-growth framing without self-improvement anxiety.
- **Phrase length:** Short and active — present tense, action verbs. Rhythm mirrors movement.
- **Vocabulary:** Physical immediacy (move, breathe, recover, feel, swim, run, trail), nature vocabulary (sea, wave, mountain, dawn, air, sun), lifestyle code (surf, yoga, trail, terrace, community), endorphin economy (energy, vitality, balance, strength).
- **Examples:** "Move. Recover. Repeat." / "Your best version lives outside." / "Performance meets paradise."

## Industry adaptations

### Real estate
Lifestyle residential with integrated sport infrastructure — running tracks, surf access, outdoor gym, wellness centre. Apartment marketing shows active residents, not interiors. Community programming (running clubs, surf schools, group yoga) as core product feature, not amenity afterthought.

### HoReCa
Sport resort or wellness hotel where movement is the primary experience architecture. Morning activity programming is the signature product. F&B is functional and energizing — smoothies, protein, restorative menus. Recovery (pools, spa, massage) as the reward loop after effort.

### Fashion
Active-lifestyle clothing at the intersection of resort and performance — sportswear that works on the trail and at the beach bar. Fabric technology explained through feel, not data. Campaign imagery always outdoors, natural light, real terrain.

### Tech
Fitness or wellness app where community is the retention mechanism — group challenges, shared goals, local running clubs. Progress visualised through body-positive metrics (energy, sleep, movement minutes), not weight or calorie obsession.

## When to apply
- Brand positions active lifestyle as the primary identity, not just an activity
- Resort, residential, or wellness product in coastal or mountain setting
- Audience is health-conscious, socially active, and values experiences over objects
- Brand needs warmth and accessibility of wellness without the austerity of zen or extreme of wild-adventure
