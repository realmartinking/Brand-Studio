## Artisan Authentic

The hands that made this are visible in the result. The brand communicates proximity — to the maker, to the source, to the process. You can almost smell it. The feeling of something real in a world of copies.

## Visual code
- **Typography:** Slightly imperfect serif or hand-lettered logotype. Letterpress feel — ink spread at stroke ends. Body text in humanist serif — Freight Text, Garamond. Never perfectly centered.
- **Palette:** Kraft, terracotta, sage, cream, earthy ochre. Colors that look like they came from pigment, not Pantone. Slightly desaturated.
- **Geometry:** Rounded and organic — no hard-edged layouts. Illustrations preferred over photography where possible. Hand-drawn elements valued.
- **Density:** Medium — enough to feel abundant, like a full market stall. Wrapping paper has information.
- **Textures:** Kraft paper, beeswax, burlap, raw linen, hand-thrown ceramic. The packaging is part of the product.

## Verbal code
- **Tone:** Warm, personal, specific. The brand speaks as the maker, not the brand manager.
- **Phrase length:** Varies — short punchy headers, then longer specific descriptions. The detail is the proof.
- **Vocabulary:** Hyper-specific ingredient/material language (Yemeni honey, Espelette pepper, 00 flour milled in Naples), process verbs (fermented, stone-ground, slow-roasted, hand-poured), first-person ("we" as the actual makers).
- **Examples:** "72-hour cold ferment. Worth it." / "The vanilla comes from one farm in Madagascar. We've visited four times." / "Made Tuesday. Eat by Friday."

## Industry adaptations

### Real estate
Small developer with one or two projects at a time. Builder's name on the website. Documentary about the construction process. Materials sourced locally and named by quarry or mill.

### HoReCa
Farm-to-table with the actual farm address on the menu. Chef does market run three times a week. Menu changes based on what was good this morning. Bread made in-house.

### Fashion
Small-batch production — 50 units per style. Maker's initials stamped inside. No restocks: when it's gone, it's gone. Natural dye colors change slightly run-to-run.

### Tech
Indie software from a small team or solo developer. Changelog written by the person who wrote the code. Patreon or direct funding model. No VC.

## When to apply
- Client has genuine maker story — actual person, actual process, actual place
- Product has sensory dimension that can be communicated (taste, texture, smell, touch)
- Audience values authenticity and is skeptical of corporate scale
- Brand benefits from "small = better" positioning
