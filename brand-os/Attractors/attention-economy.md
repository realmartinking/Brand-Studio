## Attention Economy

The brand lives in the feed. It knows the contract: you have three seconds, and if those three seconds don't deliver, you are gone. The feeling of discovering something that was made specifically for the way you consume today.

## Brands in this attractor
- [[TikTok]] (platform identity)
- [[YouTube]] (creator ecosystem)
- [[MrBeast]] (brand model)
- [[Duolingo]] (social media presence)
- [[Ryanair]] (social voice)

## Visual code
- **Typography:** Bold display type that reads in thumbnail — heavy extended grotesque, or platform-native text overlay. Hierarchy must be legible at 100px height. Captions as primary design element.
- **Palette:** High-saturation primaries — whatever cuts through the feed. Platform-compliant colors: TikTok red-black, YouTube red, Instagram gradient adjacency. Never muted in content.
- **Geometry:** No fixed system — content is the geometry. Composition driven by native platform norms (vertical 9:16, reaction face top-left, product bottom-right).
- **Density:** Maximum in content — information density creates value signal. But homepage or brand site may be clean.
- **Textures:** Screen recording quality, authentic-feeling footage, intentional imperfection as authenticity signal. Over-produced content performs less well than genuine-feeling content.

## Verbal code
- **Tone:** Immediate, conspiratorial, direct to camera. The brand speaks as if you are the only viewer, even though millions will watch.
- **Phrase length:** Extremely short or cut off mid-sentence to create continuation hook. The first word must carry weight.
- **Vocabulary:** Platform-native language (POV, storytime, wait for it, no way, actually), hook constructions ("I never expected...", "The thing nobody tells you about..."), urgency signals (now, today, before it's gone, everyone is doing this).
- **Examples:** "POV: you just discovered the trick everyone knows except you." / "We tested 47 versions. This one won." / "Stop scrolling. Seriously."

## Industry adaptations

### Real estate
Property content creators — developers who document builds on TikTok, agents who tour listings in 60 seconds. The content IS the marketing. Algorithm is the distribution.

### HoReCa
Restaurant that goes viral for one dish. Food content optimized for sharing. "Worth the queue" as brand positioning. Line outside is proof, not problem.

### Fashion
Drop culture brand where announcement is the event. Behind-the-scenes content as primary brand communication. Influencer seeding before official launch.

### Tech
App with viral loop — share this result, challenge your friends, show your score. Onboarding is a content piece. The product generates content by being used.

## When to apply
- Brand's primary growth channel is organic social
- Content virality is achievable and repeatable
- Audience is under 35 and primarily mobile-native
- Speed of iteration is a brand advantage — new content daily
