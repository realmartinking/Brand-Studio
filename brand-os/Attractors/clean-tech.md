## Clean Tech

Technology that disappears into life. The brand communicates that the best interface is one you stop thinking about. The feeling of using something that just works — and discovering you expected less.

## Brands in this attractor
- [[apple|Apple]]
- [[tesla|Tesla]] (store experience)
- [[Rivian]]
- [[Dyson]]
- [[Sonos]]

## Visual code
- **Typography:** Geometric sans-serif with humanist warmth — SF Pro (system), Helvetica Neue, Aktiv Grotesk. Always light to regular weight. Never bold in consumer communications. Hierarchy through size and color only.
- **Palette:** White, off-white, space gray, aluminum silver, midnight. One product color for variant expression. No black in consumer — deep charcoal instead.
- **Geometry:** Rounded corners everywhere — iOS-radius product language carried into all materials. Soft but precise. No hard edges in consumer-facing materials.
- **Density:** Maximum spaciousness — [[apple|Apple]]'s keynote slides have one word. Information appears only when needed.
- **Textures:** Glass, aluminum, matte plastic, ceramic. Clinical cleanliness. No grain, no texture overlays. Photography on white or light gray seamless.

## Verbal code
- **Tone:** Warm but minimal. The brand does not over-explain. Features are named, not described.
- **Phrase length:** Extremely short — often 2-4 words. The long copy lives in the technical specifications page, not the marketing.
- **Vocabulary:** Superlative but calm (thinnest, fastest, most advanced — stated flatly), product feature nouns, "just" as brand word (just works, just tap, just say).
- **Examples:** "Designed in California." / "All screen. All glass." / "The most powerful chip we've ever made."

## Industry adaptations

### Real estate
Clean-line residential developer. Digital first — app-based access, keyless entry, smart building integrated. Model apartment photographed like a product shoot on seamless white.

### HoReCa
Minimal food brand — packaging is white with one color. Flavor as the only differentiator. No chef story — the product is the story. Delivered to your door in a branded cooler bag.

### Fashion
Wearable tech or technically-functional apparel where the technology is the feature. Materials page lists fabric weight, stretch percentage, temperature range.

### Tech
Core use case. Consumer electronics or operating system. The product is the brand. Packaging is part of the brand experience. Retail environment is brand expression.

## When to apply
- Product is technology that benefits from appearing effortless
- Audience values experience over specification
- Brand competes on integration and ecosystem, not individual features
- Retail or unboxing experience is part of the value proposition
