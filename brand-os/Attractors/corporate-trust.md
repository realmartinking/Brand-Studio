## Corporate Trust

The brand communicates competence before anything else. No risk, no surprise, no personality that might alienate a single stakeholder. The feeling of being in a room where everyone is qualified and everything is under control.

## Visual code
- **Typography:** Authoritative geometric sans or transitional serif — Helvetica Neue, Sabon, ITC Garamond corporate. Consistent weight hierarchy. No decorative variation. No personality in type choices.
- **Palette:** Navy, slate gray, cool white, dark charcoal. Accent: subdued cobalt or burgundy. Maximum two colors in any layout. Color used functionally, not expressively.
- **Geometry:** Strict grid. Angular — right angles, flush alignment. No curves. Tables and charts preferred over illustration.
- **Density:** Medium — information density that signals substance without overwhelming. Every element earns its place.
- **Textures:** Matte premium paper, embossed letterhead, clean digital surfaces. No texture for aesthetic purposes — only for material quality signal.

## Verbal code
- **Tone:** Formal, third-person where possible, evidence-based. The brand does not make claims without data.
- **Phrase length:** Medium to long. Complete sentences with subordinate clauses. Precision over accessibility.
- **Vocabulary:** Institutional vocabulary (strategy, delivery, governance, framework, stakeholder), active but not expressive verbs (manage, advise, implement, ensure), numbers as authority signals.
- **Examples:** "Global advisory services for complex institutional challenges." / "17,000 professionals. 100 countries. One firm." / "Our 2024 Global Outlook is now available."

## Industry adaptations

### Real estate
Commercial developer or institutional landlord. Prospectus-format communications. ESG reporting front and center. Client testimonials from named C-suite executives.

### HoReCa
Corporate catering or private dining for business events. Service standards documented. NDA-friendly — no photography policy. Efficiency and reliability over experience and surprise.

### Fashion
Not applicable as primary. However: workwear brand targeting professional services audience. Performance fabric, minimal branding, dress-code compliant.

### Tech
Enterprise SaaS or infrastructure provider. Sales-led with procurement team as decision-maker. Compliance certifications prominently displayed. Feature list over vision statement.

## When to apply
- Client serves institutional buyers (B2B, government, professional services)
- Trust and risk-reduction are the primary purchase drivers
- Audience is skeptical of personality and authenticity signals
- Brand must survive procurement committee review
