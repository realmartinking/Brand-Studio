## Cultural Institution

The brand carries a public trust. It is not commercial in the ordinary sense — it is custodian of something that belongs to everyone. The feeling of walking into a building that exists for the common good, that holds something larger than itself.

## Visual code
- **Typography:** Clean authority — Helvetica as institutional standard, or a custom typeface that signals permanence. Modular type system. The typographic hierarchy is the wayfinding system.
- **Palette:** Primary brand color used systematically — MoMA red, Tate black. Neutrals for content. Color is institutional identifier, not expressive choice.
- **Geometry:** Angular and modular — signage system logic. Grid is infrastructure. Type and color as the primary design elements.
- **Density:** Medium — editorial richness in publication materials, clarity in physical wayfinding. The institution publishes.
- **Textures:** High-quality print on matte stock. Physical permanence. Digital presence has the same gravity as print.

## Verbal code
- **Tone:** Authoritative but welcoming. The institution assumes you are interested, not that you are already an expert. It educates without condescending.
- **Phrase length:** Variable — short for wayfinding and titles, long for wall text and publications. Both registers are equally important.
- **Vocabulary:** Cultural vocabulary (collection, exhibition, work, artist, context, dialogue), public vocabulary (all, open, community, free, everyone), institutional credibility markers (permanent, established, founded, accredited).
- **Examples:** "Free for under 18." / "Works from the collection, 1947-present." / "Art for all — not just some."

## Industry adaptations

### Real estate
Cultural destination development — museum, arts center, library. The building is a gift to the city. Public programming as ongoing obligation. Named after a benefactor or an idea, not a developer.

### HoReCa
Museum cafe or restaurant. Menu reflects the institution's cultural context. Publications and merchandise sold at the counter. The meal extends the visit.

### Fashion
Museum shop or fashion archive. Collaboration with artists from the permanent collection. The product is a cultural artifact.

### Tech
Open source project or cultural technology platform — Wikipedia, Archive.org, public library system. The institution's values are the product's values.

## When to apply
- Organization serves a genuine public cultural function
- Credibility and authority are earned through collection, programming, or scholarship
- Audience includes both experts and curious non-experts
- Brand benefits from association with permanence and public good
