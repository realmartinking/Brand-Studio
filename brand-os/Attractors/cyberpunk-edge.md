## Cyberpunk Edge

The brand exists at the intersection of machine and human, and is uncomfortable with neither. There is beauty in exposed circuitry, in industrial precision, in things that are honest about what they are. The feeling of handling something that was designed by people who care deeply about things most people ignore.

## Visual code
- **Typography:** Monospace and geometric — OCR-A, Space Mono, IBM Plex Mono. Industrial character. Type as component in a system, not decoration. High tracking, mechanical spacing.
- **Palette:** Near-black with single high-contrast accent — phosphor green, cyan, orange warning. Or inverted: white with black on light backgrounds. Colors of instrument displays and circuit boards.
- **Geometry:** Angular and precise — the tolerances of machined parts. Grid lines visible as structural elements. Exploded diagram aesthetics.
- **Density:** Medium — complex layouts that reward inspection. Specifications visible. The informed eye sees more.
- **Textures:** Anodized aluminum, PCB traces, speaker grille, milled plastic. Manufactured precision as aesthetic.

## Verbal code
- **Tone:** Matter-of-fact, technical, slightly dry. The brand respects that you know what you are looking at.
- **Phrase length:** Short, spec-driven. When longer, deeply technical. No aspiration language — only what the thing does.
- **Vocabulary:** Engineering vocabulary (tolerance, machined, signal, input, output, protocol), product specification language (Hz, ms, dB, mm, g), honest limitation language ("it does one thing extremely well").
- **Examples:** "3.5mm jack. Knobs. Faders. Nothing else." / "Replaceable by design." / "The radio receiver for those who build their own antennas."

## Industry adaptations

### Real estate
Industrial conversion with exposed structure — concrete, steel, conduit visible. Developer who publishes material specifications and construction details. Loft typology.

### HoReCa
Bar with custom lighting rig and sound system visible. Menu of technical cocktails with spec sheet (ml, ABV, dilution %). Barback equipment as decor element.

### Fashion
Technical apparel brand. Fabric data sheets. Seam construction diagrams. Performance specifications. Collab with industrial designers, not fashion stylists.

### Tech
Core use case. Hardware product, developer tool, or technical instrument. The user manual is also a design document.

## When to apply
- Product has genuine technical depth that rewards inspection
- Audience self-identifies as technically sophisticated
- Brand competes on specification and craft, not lifestyle
- Product benefits from transparency about how it works
