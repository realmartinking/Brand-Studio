## Dark Luxury

The brand activates desire through shadow rather than light. Exclusivity is felt, not stated. The feeling of something dangerous in the best possible sense — that this object or experience exists at the edge of what is permitted.

## Brands in this attractor
- [[tom-ford|Tom Ford]]
- [[Saint Laurent]] (Hedi Slimane era)
- [[Givenchy]] (Tisci era)
- [[Alexander McQueen]]
- [[Amouage]] (noir line)

## Visual code
- **Typography:** Sharp modern serif or elegant grotesque at maximum darkness — Sabon in very dark settings, custom logotypes with razor precision. White on black standard. High tracking.
- **Palette:** Near-black (not pure), deep burgundy, midnight navy, forest shadow. Gold as material accent at extreme restraint. Skin tones in photography: high contrast, low ambient light.
- **Geometry:** Angular and precise — darkness requires precision or it collapses into muddle. Clean cuts, sharp silhouettes.
- **Density:** Medium-low — the darkness creates its own visual weight. Elements placed with precision.
- **Textures:** Black leather, patent, velvet, obsidian, lacquer. Reflective surfaces that catch light selectively.

## Verbal code
- **Tone:** Low, certain, slightly dangerous. The brand does not need your approval. It invites.
- **Phrase length:** Short to medium. Declarative. Sensory and slightly ambiguous.
- **Vocabulary:** Sensory night vocabulary (shadow, depth, edge, desire, fire, skin, night), power vocabulary (authority, command, owned), understated luxury signals (the ritual, the hour, the room).
- **Examples:** "For the hour after midnight." / "The suit is a weapon." / "Desire is a discipline."

## Industry adaptations

### Real estate
Ultra-prime penthouse or private residence in a city with a nightlife culture. Photography at night, with dramatic lighting. Amenities: rooftop, private bar, screening room.

### HoReCa
Private members' bar or late-night fine dining. Dark interior with candle-only lighting. Drinks menu of extreme specificity. Entrance unmarked.

### Fashion
Luxury designer brand with evening and occasion focus. The collection is a power statement. Campaign photography: high contrast, post-midnight aesthetic.

### Tech
Not primary. However: private security or VIP services app, or luxury loyalty platform. Dark mode as default and only option.

## When to apply
- Brand targets aspirational luxury buyer who identifies with dark/night/urban aesthetic
- Product is occasion-driven and evening-coded
- Client is willing to exclude warmth-seekers in exchange for intense loyalty from target
- Sensuality and power are primary brand emotions
