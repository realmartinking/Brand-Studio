## Earth Mother

The brand believes that the choice to buy is also a choice about the planet. Activism is woven into product. The feeling of consuming without guilt — and belonging to a community that takes responsibility seriously.

## Brands in this attractor

- [[Burt's Bees]]
- [[Dr. Bronner's]]
- [[Reformation]]
- [[Allbirds]]

## Visual code
- **Typography:** Humanist sans or organic slab serif — Freight Sans, Calibre, Brandon Grotesque. Body text generously leaded. Never sterile — always readable and a little warm.
- **Palette:** Earth tones — terra cotta, sage, wheat, forest, stone, bark. Unprocessed color quality. Saturations moderate, never fluorescent. Can include soft teal or denim blue.
- **Geometry:** Rounded and organic — leaf forms, topographic lines, earth contour shapes. Hand-drawn illustration preferred over photography where budget allows.
- **Density:** Medium-low. Space for facts to breathe: environmental impact numbers, material sourcing, repair stats.
- **Textures:** Unbleached cotton, seed paper, recycled board, bark, river stone. The packaging communicates the values before the product is revealed.

## Verbal code
- **Tone:** Earnest, factual, not preachy. The brand trusts that you care — it just gives you the information.
- **Phrase length:** Medium — enough to explain the position, short enough to respect the reader.
- **Vocabulary:** Environmental vocabulary (footprint, regenerative, circular, compostable, upcycled), community we ("we believe," "we refuse," "together"), honest limitation language ("we're not perfect yet").
- **Examples:** "Don't buy this jacket." ([[patagonia|Patagonia]]) / "Made from stuff that would otherwise be in a landfill." / "1% of revenue goes to the planet. Always."

## Industry adaptations

### Real estate
Sustainable developer with certification front and center (LEED Platinum, Passivhaus). Carbon footprint of construction published. Green roof and community garden as features.

### HoReCa
Zero-waste restaurant. Seasonal menu driven by what was available at market. Composting visible. Staff can answer where every ingredient came from.

### Fashion
Transparent supply chain — factory names, wages, environmental impact per garment. Carbon-neutral shipping. Take-back program for worn items.

### Tech
Climate tech or environmental monitoring software. Impact metrics in the product dashboard. B Corp certified. Open source components.

## When to apply
- Client product has genuine environmental or social responsibility dimension
- Audience includes environmentally-motivated consumers (not just sustainability-curious)
- Brand can substantiate claims — greenwashing risk is high in this attractor
- Community and shared values are part of the value proposition
