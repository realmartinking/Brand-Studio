## Friendly Helper

The brand is genuinely on your side. It removes friction without making you feel stupid for needing help. The feeling of a smart colleague who explains things clearly and is delighted when you succeed.

## Visual code
- **Typography:** Friendly geometric sans-serif — Circular, GT Walsheim, Nunito. Medium weight. Generous line height. Illustrations integrated with type. Occasional bold for delight moments.
- **Palette:** Brand color with personality — [[mailchimp|Mailchimp]] yellow, Slack aubergine, Notion black. Supported by accessible, friendly secondaries. Never aggressive contrast. White space is generous.
- **Geometry:** Rounded corners throughout — buttons, cards, avatars, illustrations. The product feels safe to touch.
- **Density:** Comfortable medium — enough content to be useful, enough space to not feel anxious.
- **Textures:** Flat illustration with subtle texture. Emoji and illustration system used consistently. Digital-native visual language.

## Verbal code
- **Tone:** Warm, clear, occasionally witty. The brand talks with you, not at you. Humor used carefully — never at user's expense.
- **Phrase length:** Short to medium. Active voice. Second person ("you"). Direct instructions without condescension.
- **Vocabulary:** Action verbs (create, connect, send, build, share), accessible technology language, friendly contractions, occasional humor in microcopy.
- **Examples:** "Send better email." / "Where work happens." / "You did it! (And we barely helped.)" / "No jargon. Promise."

## Industry adaptations

### Real estate
Proptech for renters or first-time buyers. Clear process explained step by step. Jargon glossary in the FAQ. "We've got you covered" as positioning.

### HoReCa
Food delivery app or hospitality management tool. Friendly status updates, humor in waiting states. Staff management software that doesn't feel like enterprise software.

### Fashion
Fashion discovery or personal styling app. Onboarding questionnaire feels like a quiz, not a form. Recommendations explained in plain language. No judgment.

### Tech
Core use case. SaaS product with broad audience — not just developers. Onboarding is a strength. Help documentation is a pleasure to read. Support is fast and human.

## When to apply
- Product serves a broad audience, including non-technical users
- Onboarding and ease of adoption are differentiators
- Brand competes against established, more formal tools
- User empowerment and success are genuine brand values
