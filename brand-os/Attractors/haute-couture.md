## Haute Couture

The brand operates within a tradition so established that the codes are understood only by initiates. The ceremony is the product. The feeling of entering a world with its own language, its own pace, its own standards — and being received.

## Brands in this attractor

- [[Dior]] (Couture)
- [[Givenchy]] (atelier)
- [[Valentino]] (Couture)
- [[balenciaga|Balenciaga]] (Cristobal era)

## Visual code
- **Typography:** Geometric modern with fashion authority — Didot, Bodoni, Filosofia. Extreme contrast strokes: ultrathin horizontal, thick vertical. ALL CAPS for brand name. Razor tracking.
- **Palette:** Black and white as primary system. One signature color per season — introduced and retired. Gold as material, not color. Never casual palette.
- **Geometry:** Precise angular with feminine curve as contrast — the tension between structure and drape. Runway photography: single figure, neutral background, controlled light.
- **Density:** Sparse but intentional — the negative space is part of the composition. Nothing wasted.
- **Textures:** Silk, organza, duchess satin, point d'esprit. Photography at resolution that shows stitch detail.

## Verbal code
- **Tone:** Elevated, declarative. The brand does not pursue — it presents. You approach it.
- **Phrase length:** Short and absolute. Often a single word for the collection name. Longer only in catalogue essays.
- **Vocabulary:** Fashion system vocabulary (atelier, prêt-à-porter, maison, silhouette, proportion), season names (not Spring/Summer but named collections), "the" as qualifier (the coat, the dress, the season).
- **Examples:** "The little black dress is reinvented." / "Autumn. Structure. Light." / "Made over 600 hours in the atelier."

## Industry adaptations

### Real estate
Ultra-prime property in a fashion capital — Paris, Milan, London. The apartment named by the architect. Private viewing only. Press: one profile in a luxury architecture publication.

### HoReCa
Private dining experience connected to a fashion house or designed by a fashion creative director. Tableware custom-designed. Menu as object. Dress code.

### Fashion
Core use case. Couture, ready-to-wear luxury, or accessible luxury positioned within fashion system codes. Seasonal collection as cultural event.

### Tech
Not primary. However: fashion-tech collaboration or NFT/digital fashion — if positioned within fashion system codes rather than tech codes.

## When to apply
- Brand operates within or aspires to fashion system positioning
- Product is seasonal and event-driven
- Ceremony and anticipation are core to the consumer experience
- Audience is culturally fluent in fashion codes
