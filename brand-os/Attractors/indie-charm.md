## Indie Charm

The brand has a distinct personality that could only belong to one person or place. It is slightly off-center by design — the asymmetry is the appeal. The feeling of discovering something that is exactly your thing, that you will tell three people about.

## Visual code
- **Typography:** Eclectic mix used consistently — perhaps a mid-century sans paired with a literary serif. Type choices tell a story about the brand's taste. Custom lettering or found typography as accent.
- **Palette:** Unexpected but coherent — dusty lavender with burnt sienna, forest green with pale pink. Colors that feel like someone made a decision about them.
- **Geometry:** Slightly asymmetric, deliberately imperfect. The brand is not trying to pass a design review — it is expressing a point of view.
- **Density:** Medium — curated density. Enough to feel editorial, not enough to feel chaotic.
- **Textures:** Risograph print quality, found paper, postcard aesthetic, analog photography imperfection.

## Verbal code
- **Tone:** Personal, oblique, occasionally self-aware. The brand knows it has a sensibility and does not apologize for it.
- **Phrase length:** Variable — follows the rhythm of the content, not a rule. Can be a single evocative word or a paragraph of personality.
- **Vocabulary:** Literary references, specific proper nouns (neighborhoods, authors, films), unexpectedly precise adjectives, dry wit without cruelty.
- **Examples:** "A hotel for people who read." / "Curated by someone with opinions." / "Not for everyone. Probably for you." / "The lobby plays the kind of music you forgot you loved."

## Industry adaptations

### Real estate
Boutique residential conversion with a named creative director — an architect or designer whose sensibility pervades the project. Each unit individually styled.

### HoReCa
Cafe or bar with a clear point of view — a particular decade, a particular literature, a particular neighborhood aesthetic. The playlist, the art, the menu — all authored.

### Fashion
Small label with a signature aesthetic. Each collection is also a cultural reference. Press note reads like an essay. Bought by people who like to explain why they wear what they wear.

### Tech
Creative software or tool with a distinct personality in its UI and copy. Opinionated defaults. The tool reflects the aesthetic of the people who built it.

## When to apply
- Brand has a genuine cultural sensibility, not just visual preferences
- Audience self-selects through aesthetic recognition ("that's my kind of thing")
- Brand benefits from cult appeal over mass adoption
- Product is in creative, hospitality, or cultural space
