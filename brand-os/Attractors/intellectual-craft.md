## Intellectual Craft

The brand believes that the best design is design you eventually stop noticing. It operates at the intersection of ideas and making — where the concept and the material are equally important. An audience of curious people who read the long version.

## Visual code
- **Typography:** High-contrast transitional serif for editorial — Freight Text, Tiempos, Lyon. Monospace for details and specs — iA Writer Mono, Courier. Hierarchy through size contrast, not weight.
- **Palette:** Warm neutral base (ecru, warm gray, aged paper) with one precise accent — terracotta, slate blue, or forest green. Never more than two colors in layout.
- **Geometry:** Angular precision. Straight rules as dividers. Columns strictly aligned. Asymmetric layouts permitted when intentional.
- **Density:** Medium — enough content to feel substantive, enough space to feel editorial. 50% whitespace.
- **Textures:** Uncoated stock, textured book cloth, brushed aluminum. Touch matters — the physical artifact should feel considered.

## Verbal code
- **Tone:** Intelligent but not condescending. The brand assumes you are curious and read widely.
- **Phrase length:** Long — 20-40 words. Subordinate clauses welcome. References permitted without explanation.
- **Vocabulary:** Precise nouns (specificity over generality), craft verbs (shaped, refined, considered, assembled), temporal language (over time, slowly, decades).
- **Examples:** "The radio arrived in a box designed to be kept." / "We do not believe in versions. We believe in getting it right." / "Twelve ingredients. Four years of testing. One formula."

## Industry adaptations

### Real estate
Architecture-led developer who publishes a book about each project — materials sourced, craftspeople named, decisions explained. The apartment is secondary to the thought behind it.

### HoReCa
Restaurant where the chef writes a monthly essay on sourcing. Menu lists farm and region, not just ingredient. The story is on the table before the food arrives.

### Fashion
Brand that publishes the weight of every fabric in grams per square meter. Stitches counted. Provenance mapped. Seasonal collection is also a research document.

### Tech
Developer tool or creative software where documentation is a joy to read. Blog posts that explain the thinking. The product changelog reads like a letter.

## When to apply
- Client has genuine craft story and is willing to share process
- Audience has high education and is attracted to depth
- Brand competes on quality of thought, not price or reach
- Product has long lifespan and benefits from "discovery over time"
