## Modern Premium

The brand has outgrown the need to shout. Quality is self-evident in execution. The feeling of something that cost real money and was worth it — not because of the logo, but because of what it does and how it does it.

## Visual code
- **Typography:** Clean contemporary serif or premium grotesque — Neue Montreal, GT Sectra, Graphik. Medium weight. Tracking slightly tight. Size hierarchy creates tension without drama.
- **Palette:** Sophisticated neutrals with one premium material reference — platinum, titanium, deep blue-black, forest green. Never looks cheap, never tries hard.
- **Geometry:** Precise angular — edges are sharp because the manufacturing allows it. Tolerances matter. Every curve is calculated.
- **Density:** Medium-low — enough to communicate substance, not enough to feel uncertain.
- **Textures:** Anodized aluminum, matte glass, premium leather, cold-rolled steel. The physical product texture is the brand texture.

## Verbal code
- **Tone:** Confident without arrogance. Informed without lecturing. Speaks to a peer, not a customer.
- **Phrase length:** Medium — complete thoughts, no padding. Respects the reader's time.
- **Vocabulary:** Engineering and craft vocabulary (precision, engineered, refined, optimized), performance adjectives (exceptional, uncompromising, enduring), material nouns (aluminum, glass, leather, steel).
- **Examples:** "Engineered for those who notice the difference." / "Nothing added. Nothing removed." / "Two years in development. Built to last twenty."

## Industry adaptations

### Real estate
Premium residential — not ultra-luxury, but demonstrably superior to standard. Specification sheet as hero content. Architect named and explained. Material choices justified.

### HoReCa
Design hotel or premium casual restaurant. The fit-out is a statement. Service standard is high but not stiff. Wine list curated, not comprehensive. Staff knowledgeable without being scripted.

### Fashion
Investment wardrobe brand. Capsule approach. Technical fabric meets refined cut. No seasonal trend — buy it now, wear it in ten years.

### Tech
Hardware product where the physical quality matches the software quality. Packaging is satisfying to open. Documentation designed to the same standard as the UI.

## When to apply
- Client product has demonstrable quality advantage over category average
- Audience is informed and values execution over image
- Brand needs to justify 2-3x price premium over standard
- Purchase is considered and infrequent — buyer researches thoroughly
