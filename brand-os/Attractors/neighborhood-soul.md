## Neighborhood Soul

The brand belongs to a place. It is not trying to be anywhere else. The feeling of walking in and being recognized — not famous, just known. The third place: not home, not work, but the place between them.

## Visual code
- **Typography:** Warm humanist sans or hand-lettered — Process Type Foundry, Freight Sans, or something that looks like it was drawn by someone who also works here. Type on chalkboard permitted.
- **Palette:** Warm and neighborhood-specific — the colors of the local building materials. Terracotta, warm gray, forest, brick. Never corporate.
- **Geometry:** Rounded and lived-in. The furniture has been rearranged multiple times. Organic layout in physical space.
- **Density:** Medium — enough to feel lively. Community notices on the board. Handwritten specials.
- **Textures:** Wood counters worn smooth, blackboard paint, pinboard cork, reclaimed brick. Imperfection as authenticity.

## Verbal code
- **Tone:** Personal, immediate, knowing. The brand speaks as a neighbor, not a brand manager.
- **Phrase length:** Conversational — whatever the situation calls for. A warm "see you tomorrow" or a paragraph about where the coffee comes from.
- **Vocabulary:** Place vocabulary (here, neighborhood, local, corner, across the street), community vocabulary (regulars, staff names used publicly, "we're"), present tense and current events ("this week," "today," "Tuesday's").
- **Examples:** "Your corner. Since 2009." / "We know your order." / "Owned by the neighborhood. More or less."

## Industry adaptations

### Real estate
Community-embedded mixed-use development. Ground floor retail designed for independent operators. The developer stays and becomes a landlord who knows tenants by name. Local art program.

### HoReCa
Third-wave coffee shop or neighborhood restaurant with deep local relationships. Staff are community members. Events: poetry nights, book clubs, local producer pop-ups.

### Fashion
Local boutique with national online presence. Personal styling by appointment. The store is a gathering place.

### Tech
Community platform or local discovery app. Hyperlocal by design. Neighborhood as the organizing logic.

## When to apply
- Brand is genuinely rooted in a specific place or community
- Proximity and personal relationship are brand differentiators
- Audience values local over global, independent over chain
- Brand serves a "third place" function — between home and work
