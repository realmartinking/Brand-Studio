## Nostalgic Comfort

The brand transports you to a version of the past that feels safe. Not history — memory. The feeling of a kitchen that smelled like something baking, a grandmother's tablecloth, the particular light of a Sunday afternoon decades ago.

## Visual code
- **Typography:** Rounded slab serif or warm grotesque from mid-century — Clarendon, Rockwell, Cooper Black for display. Body text in friendly humanist sans — Freight Sans, Milo. Letterforms slightly heavier than expected.
- **Palette:** Faded tones — dusty rose, warm mustard, sage green, milk white, burnt sienna. As if left in sunlight for a decade. Never fresh, never neon.
- **Geometry:** Rounded shapes predominate — oval labels, arched frames, curved corners on all rectangular elements. Decorative borders permitted.
- **Density:** Medium — layered vintage textures, stamp marks, grain. Not overwhelming, but never empty.
- **Textures:** Aged paper, faded linen, enameled surfaces, worn wood. Grain film overlay in photography.

## Verbal code
- **Tone:** Warm and slightly wistful. The brand gently reminds you of something you thought you'd forgotten.
- **Phrase length:** Medium — conversational, no jargon. A sentence that your grandmother would understand and appreciate.
- **Vocabulary:** Domestic nouns (kitchen, hearth, Sunday, table, recipe), sensory memory triggers (smell, warmth, soft, familiar), time-worn verbs (remember, return, keep, share).
- **Examples:** "The recipe hasn't changed since your mother's kitchen." / "Some things are better the second time." / "Made for kitchens that have seen things."

## Industry adaptations

### Real estate
Residential development that restores rather than replaces. Original parquet floors refinished, not covered. Archive photography of former residents. "Lives lived here" as the conceptual thread.

### HoReCa
Diner or cafe with deliberate mid-century decor. Classic dishes with current ingredients. Regulars know the staff names. Physical menu laminated with vintage aesthetic.

### Fashion
Archival reissue brand — exact reproductions of historic pieces with provenance stories. Or slow fashion brand using deadstock fabrics from 1970s-1990s mills.

### Tech
App with skeuomorphic details — not ironically, genuinely. Digital journaling tool that feels like a physical notebook. The interface rewards slowness.

## When to apply
- Client product or service connects to domestic/personal memory
- Audience demographic has strong connection to a specific decade (often 20-40 years prior)
- Brand wants to evoke safety and familiarity without being generic
- Product is comfort-category: food, home, personal care
