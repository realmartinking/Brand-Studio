## Old Money

The brand signals that wealth so established it no longer needs to announce itself. No logos visible at a distance. The people who recognize it are the only ones who matter. Understated as a form of supremacy.

## Visual code
- **Typography:** Formal transitional or old-style serif — Caslon, Bembo, Garamond. Never grotesque. Engraving quality — thin strokes, refined serifs. Small caps where appropriate.
- **Palette:** Camel, oyster, champagne, hunter green, midnight navy. All colors that look like they belong to a private members' club or a country estate. Zero synthetic tones.
- **Geometry:** Restrained angular with historical references — traditional heraldic forms, subtle crest elements, oval cartouche shapes. No modernism.
- **Density:** Low-medium — generous whitespace, but pages are not empty. Information present but unhurried.
- **Textures:** Woven tweed, cashmere, cold-press paper, polished silver, oak paneling. The brand smells like a private library.

## Verbal code
- **Tone:** Assured, slightly archaic. The brand does not explain itself. It simply states.
- **Phrase length:** Short to medium. Declarative. No exclamation marks. No questions.
- **Vocabulary:** Heritage nouns (estate, family, provenance, lineage, tradition), material aristocracy (cashmere, flannel, silver, walnut), temporal permanence (since, always, for generations, enduring).
- **Examples:** "Since 1849." / "For those who require no introduction." / "The coat your son will wear."

## Industry adaptations

### Real estate
Ultra-prime residential in established neighborhoods. No brochure — only private viewings by introduction. Price available upon request. The postcode is the brand.

### HoReCa
Private members' dining. No website. Membership by nomination. Menu changes daily, communicated verbally. The bill is handled before you arrive.

### Fashion
Made-to-measure with 12-week lead time. Fabrics sourced from mills that have supplied the brand for 40 years. No seasonal campaign — client base is self-renewing.

### Tech
Not applicable as primary. However: wealth management platform for UHNW individuals. UI is spare, conservative, not designed to impress — designed to inform.

## When to apply
- Client targets ultra-high-net-worth or aspirational-wealthy audience
- Product is at the highest price point in category with no promotional activity
- Brand benefits from inaccessibility as a feature, not a bug
- Heritage, provenance, or place is genuinely part of the product
