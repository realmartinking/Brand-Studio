## Pop Culture

The brand has achieved something rare: genuine cultural ubiquity without cynicism. It does not explain itself because explanation would break the spell. The feeling of something that has always been part of the landscape.

## Brands in this attractor
- [[Coca-Cola]]
- [[mcdonalds|McDonald's]]
- [[Lego]]
- [[Nike]] (core, not premium)
- [[Disney]]

## Visual code
- **Typography:** Custom logotype as primary visual element — proprietary, instantly recognizable. Secondary type: clean, accessible grotesque. Legibility at all sizes, all media.
- **Palette:** Signature brand color owns a single hue — Coca-Cola red, McDonald's yellow, Lego red. Color is trademark. Single primary with white and black.
- **Geometry:** Rounded and friendly — arc, circle, stadium shape. Brand mark works at 10px and on a building facade.
- **Density:** Low-medium — the logo does the work. No complex layouts in primary communications.
- **Textures:** Flat, digital-native. Highly reproducible on any surface, any medium. No texture dependency.

## Verbal code
- **Tone:** Universally warm and optimistic. Never alienates. The brand is for everyone and sounds like it.
- **Phrase length:** Very short — often a single imperative. The tagline is a sentence of five words or fewer that lasts decades.
- **Vocabulary:** Universal nouns (life, happiness, joy, family, world, imagination), simple action verbs (share, play, taste, feel, go), pronouns that include rather than select.
- **Examples:** "Just Do It." / "I'm Lovin' It." / "The happiest place on earth." / "Open Happiness."

## Industry adaptations

### Real estate
Mass-market residential developer with national reach. Clear lifestyle aspirations — home ownership as universal value. Primary colors, open layouts, "for everyone" messaging.

### HoReCa
QSR (quick service restaurant) or global chain. Consistency across every location as brand promise. Value-for-money with emotional warmth. Family occasion positioning.

### Fashion
Mass-market fashion brand — global, democratic, instantly recognizable. Collaboration strategy with pop culture figures. Limited editions create desire within democratic base.

### Tech
Consumer app with billions of users. Onboarding in under 30 seconds. Localized into every major language. The UI is the brand.

## When to apply
- Brand targets the broadest possible demographic
- Market share and volume are primary objectives
- Brand needs to sustain cultural relevance through decades, not seasons
- Product is consumed frequently — daily or weekly
