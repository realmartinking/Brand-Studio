## Provocateur

The brand exists to make you uncomfortable, and that discomfort is the value. Not shock for shock's sake — conceptual provocation that reveals something true about the present moment. The audience is not everyone, and the brand is fine with that.

## Visual code
- **Typography:** Distorted, corrupted, or hyper-institutional. Either broken grotesque with tracking extremes, or DIN-style bureaucratic neutrality used ironically. System fonts used deliberately. CAPS or all-lowercase, rarely mixed.
- **Palette:** Either total grayscale with one violent color injection, or uncanny pastels. Colors that feel wrong: hospital green, safety orange, autopsy blue.
- **Geometry:** Deconstructed — familiar shapes broken apart. Asymmetry as philosophy. The grid used only to be violated.
- **Density:** Medium — selective density. One element at extreme scale, rest of space empty.
- **Textures:** Industrial, clinical, or consumer-culture references. Shrink wrap, security tape, price sticker aesthetics, brutalist concrete.

## Verbal code
- **Tone:** Flat, clinical, or absurdist. Zero emotional appeal. The brand does not want you to like it.
- **Phrase length:** Either one word or a paragraph-long conceptual statement. No middle ground.
- **Vocabulary:** Institutional language used with sinister precision, brand jargon turned against itself, consumer culture vocabulary inverted.
- **Examples:** "EVERYTHING." / "We have been selected to announce the winner of consumer choice award for best product in the category of life." / "Not a shoe. A document."

## Industry adaptations

### Real estate
Conceptual developer who publishes a manifesto about what the city has lost. Building design deliberately against local aesthetic. Name is a statement, not an address.

### HoReCa
Restaurant where the concept is the experience and the food is secondary. Menu is an object. Eating is a performance. You may not understand it until three days later.

### Fashion
Brand that treats each collection as a cultural critique. Press release is a theoretical text. Collaborates with artists, not celebrities. Retail environment is a gallery.

### Tech
Product that does something obvious in a deliberately strange way. The strangeness reveals something about how you assumed technology should work. User manual is a philosophical text.

## When to apply
- Brand is willing to lose mainstream audience to gain intense loyalty from a niche
- Client has genuine conceptual or artistic position, not just an "edgy" aesthetic
- Product exists in art, fashion, or culture space where provocation has market value
- Brand timeline is event-driven, not always-on
