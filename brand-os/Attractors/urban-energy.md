## Urban Energy

The brand runs at the speed of the city. Constant drop culture, immediate relevance, community as currency. The feeling of being in on something before it breaks through. Heat, noise, presence.

## Visual code
- **Typography:** Heavy extended grotesque or display type — Druk, Neue Haas Grotesk Bold, custom block letters. ALL CAPS. Overlapping type layers. Text used as image.
- **Palette:** High-contrast primaries — red, yellow, black, white. Or unexpected color combinations that feel wrong but look right. No muted tones. Color used as impact.
- **Geometry:** Angular and dense. No whitespace as luxury — whitespace is wasted opportunity. Box-heavy compositions. Stacked elements.
- **Density:** High — information everywhere, all at once. Layered graphics, competing elements, controlled chaos.
- **Textures:** Spray paint on brick, heat transfer on cotton, screen print grain, neon on asphalt. Industrial surfaces and fast production methods as aesthetic.

## Verbal code
- **Tone:** Insider code. The brand speaks to those who are already in the know. No explanation.
- **Phrase length:** Extremely short or no copy at all. A single word, a date, a number. Or dense paragraphs of stream-of-consciousness.
- **Vocabulary:** Current slang, place names as status markers (downtown, the block, the city), intentional misspellings, numbers with cultural meaning.
- **Examples:** "DROP. THURSDAY. 11AM." / "Not for everyone." / "If you know, you know."

## Industry adaptations

### Real estate
Urban loft-to-residential conversion in creative district. Graffiti mural commissioned and preserved. Building name is a number. Proximity to creative scene as primary selling point.

### HoReCa
Late-night venue. No signage. Menu on chalkboard, changes nightly. DJ set starts at midnight. Queue is part of the experience. Collaboration with local artists monthly.

### Fashion
Drop model — limited units, no restocks, announced 48 hours in advance. Collaboration-heavy. No seasonal collection — only events. Community waitlist.

### Tech
App launched in one city first. Invite-only for six months. "Built in [city]" as identity element. Rapid iteration visible — version numbers increment weekly.

## When to apply
- Brand targets urban youth culture (18-28)
- Scarcity and exclusivity create demand without luxury pricing
- Community and cultural credibility matter more than product specification
- Brand benefits from drop/event model rather than always-available retail
