## Warm Heritage

Time is the main ingredient. The brand communicates that what it makes cannot be rushed — and that is the point. The feeling of holding something that will outlast you: gravity, warmth, earned trust.

## Visual code
- **Typography:** Old-style serif with organic stroke contrast — Caslon, Garamond Premier, Freight Display. Slightly condensed. Ink trap details visible at large sizes. Initials and monograms used as structural elements.
- **Palette:** Saddle tan, cognac, deep burgundy, forest green, aged ivory. Gold as accent — never silver. Never pure black — use very dark brown or navy.
- **Geometry:** Organic angular — traditional shield forms, heraldic references, oval lozenges. Nothing that looks like it was drawn in software.
- **Density:** Medium — enough visual material to feel rich, not overwhelming. Stories layered in.
- **Textures:** Leather, wax, aged paper, linen, burnished metal. Patina is a feature, not a flaw.

## Verbal code
- **Tone:** Unhurried, assured. The brand speaks as a craftsman who has seen trends come and go.
- **Phrase length:** Medium to long. Complete thoughts. Occasional archaisms used deliberately.
- **Vocabulary:** Craft terms (tempered, hand-stitched, seasoned, aged), time markers (since, generations, decades, centuries), material heritage (calfskin, waxed canvas, sterling, grain leather).
- **Examples:** "The same last since 1891." / "Hand-welted. Not because it is faster." / "Your grandfather's watch. Now yours."

## Industry adaptations

### Real estate
Historic building conversion where original features — stone floors, ironwork, plaster moldings — are documented and preserved. Developer publishes archival photographs. Naming reflects location history.

### HoReCa
Restaurant in a heritage building with preserved service traditions. Sommelier with three decades of experience. The same bread recipe since opening. Wine list organized by producer family history.

### Fashion
Brand that publishes the name of the artisan who made the piece. Repair service included. Seasonal catalogue is also a history of materials.

### Tech
Software company founded decades ago, still maintained by original team. No pivots. Changelog stretches back to version 1.0. The manual is a physical book.

## When to apply
- Client has genuine history (20+ years) or can credibly borrow heritage from place or craft tradition
- Product is acquired, not consumed — meant to be kept
- Audience values provenance and is skeptical of novelty
- Brand needs to justify premium price through time and craft, not innovation
