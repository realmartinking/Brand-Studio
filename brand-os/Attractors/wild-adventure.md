## Wild Adventure

The brand respects the terrain more than it respects convention. Performance is not claimed — it is proven. The feeling of being equipped for something real, something that requires preparation and demands respect.

## Visual code
- **Typography:** Sturdy condensed grotesque or slab serif — Tungsten, Eurostile, Rockwell Condensed. Reads clearly at distance and in motion. Weight: bold to black. No fine serifs that would be lost on texture.
- **Palette:** Functional color-coding — safety orange, summit gold, deep forest, midnight navy, slate gray. Secondary palette of earthy desaturated tones. Color choices made for visibility and durability, not trend.
- **Geometry:** Angular — triangles (mountains), horizontal bands (horizon lines), diagonal momentum. Brand marks are compact and reproducible on every surface.
- **Density:** Medium — enough information to demonstrate preparation. Technical specifications visible but not overwhelming.
- **Textures:** Weathered canvas, Cordura nylon, Dyneema, rubber, GoreTex. The product materials are the brand materials.

## Verbal code
- **Tone:** Direct, earned, factual. The brand does not oversell. If it says waterproof, it means waterproof.
- **Phrase length:** Short to medium. Imperative mood used confidently.
- **Vocabulary:** Terrain vocabulary (summit, trail, ridge, base camp, expedition), performance specifications (waterproof rating, insulation fill power, pack weight), earned verbs (tested, proven, built for, designed to last).
- **Examples:** "There's no bad weather." / "Built for the mountain, tested by it." / "5,000 meters. Still dry."

## Industry adaptations

### Real estate
Rugged residential in a remote or mountainous location. Engineering of the building as the story — thermal performance, wind resistance, orientation to landscape. The environment is the feature.

### HoReCa
Expedition catering, adventure tour operator's restaurant, or mountain hut cuisine. Simple, high-calorie, honest food. No tablecloths. Menu written on chalkboard. Staff wear the gear they sell.

### Fashion
Technical outdoor apparel. Every garment has a use case. Tag shows technical specifications. Repairability built in. Collaboration with actual mountaineers and guides.

### Tech
GPS, tracking, or field communication product. Durable hardware rated for extreme conditions. Interface usable with gloves on. Battery life measured in weeks not hours.

## When to apply
- Client product is used in outdoor, physical, or demanding-environment contexts
- Brand needs to communicate reliability and preparedness, not style
- Audience self-identifies as active and values functional performance
- Product benefits from evidence of testing in real conditions
