## Zen Wellness

The brand creates the conditions for stillness. Not productivity — presence. The feeling of arriving somewhere that exists outside of ordinary time, where your nervous system understands that nothing is urgent here.

## Visual code
- **Typography:** Ultra-light serif or hairline grotesque — Canela Thin, Campton Extralight, Optima. Letters spaced generously. Never crowded. Text appears to float.
- **Palette:** Stone, water, ash, fog, rice paper, chalk. The palette of surfaces after rain. Zero saturation. Cool neutrals leaning warm — never purely cold.
- **Geometry:** Rounded without being soft — water-worn stone forms, circular meditation mandalas, oval compositions. Symmetry as rest.
- **Density:** Maximum spaciousness. Single elements in vast negative space. If text, three words maximum on a visual.
- **Textures:** Undyed linen, polished river stone, matte ceramics, raw wood grain. Surfaces you want to touch slowly.

## Verbal code
- **Tone:** Gentle, present-tense, sensory. The brand speaks at the pace of an exhale.
- **Phrase length:** Short and unhurried. Often a single instruction, a single sensation.
- **Vocabulary:** Sensory immediacy (breathe, feel, arrive, rest, release), temporal present ("now," "here," "this moment"), body vocabulary (skin, breath, weight, warmth, quiet).
- **Examples:** "Arrive." / "The ritual begins at the door." / "One hour. Nothing else."

## Industry adaptations

### Real estate
Wellness residential concept — spa facilities included, meditation garden, sleep-focused unit design, acoustic insulation prioritized. Architecture uses biophilic design principles.

### HoReCa
Spa, retreat center, or wellness cafe. No music or 40bpm ambient. Natural light prioritized. Menu is botanical and functional. The ritual of ordering is slow by design.

### Fashion
Slow fashion with clothing designed for being rather than doing — loungewear, meditation wear, sleepwear as lifestyle category. Fabrics described by touch.

### Tech
Wellness or mindfulness app. No notification badges. Dark mode. Analog-feeling interface. Breathing exercises as onboarding. Usage limits encouraged in the product.

## When to apply
- Client product is in wellness, spa, retreat, or mindfulness category
- Brand explicitly positions against overstimulation and urgency
- Audience is seeking relief from information density
- Ritual and experience design are core to the value proposition
