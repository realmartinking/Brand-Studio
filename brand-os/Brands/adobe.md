## Adobe

Adobe Inc. was founded in 1982 in Mountain View, California by John Warnock and Charles Geschke, originally focused on PostScript and desktop publishing. Today it is the dominant platform for professional creative work: Photoshop (1988), Illustrator, InDesign, Premiere Pro, After Effects, and the cloud ecosystem Adobe Creative Cloud (launched 2013) comprise the industry standard toolkit for designers, photographers, videographers, and marketers worldwide. The tagline "Creativity for all" signals a strategic expansion from professionals toward a broader creative audience, including the Express product line for non-professionals.

## Why this position

**participation 0.8:** Adobe's products exist solely to enable creation — the brand's output is other people's creative work. This is participation at maximum: every piece of design, film, photograph, or document made with Adobe tools is brand expression by proxy.

**nature_technology 0.8:** Adobe is a technology company whose entire value proposition is creative and expressive output. The tension between the coldness of software and the warmth of human creativity is the core brand dynamic — "Creativity for all" resolves it toward the human end, but the infrastructure is deeply technological.

**all_few 0.3:** Creative Cloud subscriptions serve tens of millions of users across education, freelance, agency, and enterprise segments. The "for all" positioning since 2019 pushes further toward inclusion. Price points remain above mass-market but below luxury-software territory.

**control_release 0.7:** Adobe's ecosystem is highly controlled — the Creative Cloud subscription lock, the proprietary file formats (.psd, .ai, .indd), the certification system. Users invest deeply in the Adobe environment, making switching costly. The brand is generous with creative possibility while being architecturally controlling.

**body_mind 0.8:** The output of creative software is visual, sensory, embodied (film, photography, illustration) — but the tools themselves are cognitively demanding. High mind engagement through mastery, high body engagement through the visual output.

## Visual code

- **Typography:** Adobe Clean (proprietary, designed in-house) — a humanist sans with a warm grotesque quality; used across all product UIs and marketing. Medium to bold weight. Approachable but professional.
- **Palette:** Adobe Red (#FF0000 / #FA0F00) — one of the most saturated brand reds in enterprise software. Used on the logo and primary CTA contexts. Dark charcoal (#1F1F1F) and white for UI contexts. Product sub-brands have their own colour codes (Photoshop blue, Illustrator orange, Premiere purple).
- **Geometry:** The Adobe logo mark is an angular stylised "A" — geometric and precise. Product icons follow a consistent grid with rounded-square containers. UI is component-based, grid-driven.
- **Density:** Medium-high in product UI (complex tool panels, multi-layer interfaces); clean and focused in marketing (hero image, product screen, single CTA).
- **Textures:** Flat digital throughout. Creative showcase materials use maximum visual richness (user-generated artwork), contrasting with the clean white brand system.
