## Aesop

Skincare brand from Melbourne, built on the idea that beauty ritual is a form of intellectual practice. Every store is individually designed by a local architect. Every product description reads like a literature excerpt. The amber bottle is the silhouette — not the logo.

## Why this position

Self-community 0.3: Aesop is about personal ritual — the morning routine, the bathroom as private sanctuary. Community exists only among those who recognise the bottle.

Control-release 0.8: Every touchpoint is rigorously controlled — store design briefs, copy tone, product naming. Nothing is random.

Body-mind 0.6: The product is physical (skin, scent, texture) but the brand is intensely conceptual. Literary quotes on walls, botanical nomenclature on labels. The mind is required.

All-few 0.7: Not mass — the price point and the communication register filter. But not ultra-exclusive either — it sits at the premium tier, reachable but deliberate.

Loud-quiet 0.9: Aesop almost never shouts. No celebrity campaigns. No seasonal promotions. The silence is the statement.

Nature-technology 0.3: Botanical ingredients are the foundation. The brand codes as plant-derived, research-backed, not industrial.

Rhythm 0.8 / Speed 0.7: The store experience is deliberately unhurried. You are offered a product to try, a scent to consider. The ritualisation of the transaction is the brand.

Repeatability 0.2: Daily skincare routine — deeply habitual product in a non-habitual brand wrapper.

## Visual code
- **Typography:** Garamond or Freight Text — a literary serif that reads like a book, not a billboard. Wide leading, moderate tracking. No bold. The text IS the design.
- **Palette:** Umber, raw sienna, ecru, forest green, muted terracotta. Amber glass as recurring chromatic object. No white-white — always warm off-white.
- **Geometry:** Rounded-organic — the bottle is cylindrical, labels are centered, no aggressive angles. The store architecture varies by location but always references natural form.
- **Density:** Low density in print — one product per frame, extensive white space. Stores are dense with objects but never cluttered.
- **Textures:** Amber glass, kraft paper, raw timber shelving, unglazed ceramic, linen. Every material that develops a patina over time.

## Verbal code
- **Tone:** Erudite, unhurried, slightly wry. Speaks as a well-read friend who happens to know botany and literature equally well.
- **Phrase length:** Medium to long — subordinate clauses, considered rhythm, never a slogan.
- **Vocabulary:** Botanical nomenclature (Citrus aurantium, Melaleuca alternifolia), literary allusions, sensory precision (astringent, resinous, desiccating), philosophy of care.
- **Examples:** "A formulation imbued with purpose and olfactory pleasure." / "The bathroom need not be a site of haste." / "Aromatically complex, with a medicinal frankness that rewards attention."
