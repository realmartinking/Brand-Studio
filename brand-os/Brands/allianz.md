## Allianz

Allianz SE was founded in 1890 in Munich, Germany, and today operates as one of the world's largest financial services companies, present in over 70 countries with approximately 150,000 employees. Its core businesses are property/casualty insurance, life/health insurance, and asset management (PIMCO, Allianz Global Investors). The brand is institutionally oriented — major stadium naming rights (Allianz Arena Munich, Allianz Riviera Nice, Allianz Stadium Turin) signal scale and permanence. The tagline "The trusted partner" communicates the core brand promise: reliability under risk.

## Why this position

**control_release 0.9:** Insurance is the business of control — managing, pricing, and absorbing risk. Allianz's brand communicates that it has everything under control so you don't have to. Maximum predictability, zero surprise in brand communication.

**serious_playful 0.1:** No playfulness in this brand. Claims, policies, actuarial tables, regulatory compliance. The category itself resists levity, and Allianz leans into institutional gravity rather than fighting it.

**distance 0.8:** Formal relationship between brand and customer. Communication is structured, professional, uses third-person framing. The brand does not befriend — it partners.

**body_mind 0.8:** Insurance is fundamentally mind-oriented — risk calculation, future-state planning, protection of abstract assets. Unlike product brands, there is no physical sensation of purchase.

**temperature 0.7:** Cool blue dominates the palette — rational, trustworthy, not warm. The eagle logo reinforces authority over intimacy.

**repeatability 0.2:** Insurance policies are long-duration commitments, not daily interactions. Customer touchpoints are infrequent (annual renewal, claims).

## Visual code

- **Typography:** A custom geometric sans-serif (introduced in the 2006 Interbrand refresh, later systematised) — clean, authoritative, no decorative detail. Headlines are medium weight with generous tracking. Body copy is restrained.
- **Palette:** Allianz Blue (#003781) as primary, white as base. Light blue (#00B0CA) used as accent in digital contexts. No warm tones in brand system — the palette signals cool reliability.
- **Geometry:** The Allianz eagle logo is a bold, abstracted heraldic form — angular and symmetrical. Layout systems use strict grid, right-angle compositions, no decorative curves.
- **Density:** Medium — information-forward (policy details, coverage summaries) but not visually cluttered. Every element serves a functional purpose.
- **Textures:** Clean matte digital surfaces; print materials use premium coated stock. No decorative textures — the brand is about substance, not surface.

## Verbal code

- **Tone:** Authoritative, calm, evidence-based. The brand does not over-promise — it states. Reassurance through competence, not warmth.
- **Phrase length:** Medium to long — complete sentences, precise qualifications. Clarity over brevity when accuracy matters.
- **Vocabulary:** Protection vocabulary (secure, protect, safeguard, cover), institutional nouns (partnership, commitment, expertise, stability), scale signals (global, leading, trusted, 130 years).
- **Examples:** "The trusted partner." / "With Allianz, you're in good hands." / "We secure your future." / "Engineered for those who plan ahead."
