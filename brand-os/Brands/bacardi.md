## Bacardi

Founded 1862 in Santiago de Cuba by Facundo Bacardí Massó. The original distillery in Cuba had fruit bats living in the rafters — the bat became the brand's enduring symbol and good luck charm. Exiled from Cuba after the 1959 revolution, now headquartered in Hamilton, Bermuda. World's largest privately held spirits company. Bacardi rum is the base of the Mojito and Daiquiri — cocktail culture is the brand's natural habitat. Landor has worked on the Bacardi identity system.

## Why this position

Control-release 0.2: Maximum release orientation. Bacardi is fundamentally a party brand — the product lowers inhibitions, the marketing celebrates letting go. "Do What Moves You" embodies this.

Temperature 0.8: Tropical warmth is the brand's native climate — Cuban heritage, Caribbean rum, beach bars, summer nights. The warmest major spirits brand.

Body-mind 0.7: Sensory and physical — the taste, the warmth, the social energy. Bacardi's brand experience is felt in the body first.

Participation 0.6: Social and participatory by nature. Rum is almost always mixed, shared, consumed in groups. Cocktail culture is collaborative.

Serious-playful 0.2: Maximally playful and celebratory. The bat mascot, the party imagery, the cocktail recipes — no gravitas, all energy.
