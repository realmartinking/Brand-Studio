## Burger King

Burger King is the second-largest fast food hamburger chain globally, founded in 1953, headquartered in Miami (parent company: Restaurant Brands International). In 2021, Jones Knowles Ritchie executed a landmark rebrand — the brand's first major visual identity update in over 20 years — returning to a retro aesthetic inspired by Burger King's 1969-1994 visual era. The rebrand introduced Bressay (a custom rounded serif typeface), a warming color palette of brown, orange, red, and cream, and a simplified, appetizing visual system that prioritizes food photography and analog warmth over digital flatness. The rebrand was widely recognized as one of the most significant fast-food rebrands of the decade.

## Why this position

- **roots_horizon: 0.2** — The 2021 JKR rebrand is explicitly and deliberately nostalgic. It references 1969 Burger King visual language. The design decision to look backward is the strategy — authenticity through heritage versus McDonald's contemporary design direction.
- **temperature: 0.1** — The warmest brand palette in QSR. Brown, orange, flame red, golden cream. The colors are food colors — the palette literally looks like the food on the tray. JKR's color decision is about appetite appeal through warmth.
- **body_mind: 0.8** — "Flame-grilled" is a visceral, physical, bodily proposition. The taste, the char, the aroma. BK's positioning is sensory pleasure over any rational or experiential claim. The body is the target.
- **geometry: 0.7** — Bressay typeface has rounded, bun-like curves. The logo bun motif with the wordmark inside is rounded and organic. The whole visual system is curves, arcs, and soft forms — echoing the food itself.
- **distance: 0.1** — "Have it your way" (reactivated in 2021 messaging) is radical personalization promise. 40+ Whopper customizations. The brand's promise is individual accommodation.

- **Typography:** Bressay — a custom rounded serif typeface designed by JKR for the 2021 rebrand. Inspired by 1970s food typography, with ink-trap details and a deliberately analog, pre-digital character. Display use at large scale; functional use at menu sizes.
