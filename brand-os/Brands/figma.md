## Figma

Founded 2012 by Dylan Field and Evan Wallace. First design tool to run entirely in the browser — eliminating the version control problem of native software. The multiplayer cursor became the product's iconic image: multiple designers, real-time, same file. Adobe acquired Figma for $20 billion in 2022; the deal was blocked by regulators in 2023. The brand made collaborative design the default expectation for an entire generation of product designers.

## Why this position

Participation 0.9: The highest co-creation score in this dataset. Figma's product is literally built for simultaneous multi-user creation. The cursor of another designer appearing in your file is the brand's most iconic moment. The product does not work as a solo tool in the same way — collaboration is structural.

Roots-horizon 0.8: Figma arrived when design tools were desktop-native and file-based. The browser-native, real-time approach was genuinely ahead of the industry. The brand continues to define where design tooling moves.

All-few 0.3: Freemium model — free for individual designers, paid for teams. Professional tool but genuinely accessible. The free tier was instrumental in building adoption among students and independent designers.

Nature-technology 0.8: Entirely digital. The product has no physical dimension. The brand communicates through digital outputs — design systems, prototypes, shared libraries.

Rhythm 0.3 / Speed 0.3: Design work is iterative and fast — rapid prototyping, instant sharing, same-day feedback cycles. The product accelerates the design process.

Repeatability 0.1: Daily tool use — Figma is open most of the working day for its users. Maximum routine frequency.

Body-mind 0.7: Design is intellectual work (systems thinking, problem-solving) but the output is visual and aesthetic — closer to mind but with sensory output dimension.

Loud-quiet 0.4: The brand has personality — Config (annual conference) is culturally significant in the design community. But the product UI is restrained and the marketing is not aggressive.

## Attractor difference

Creator-economy attractor: self=0.4, co-created=0.9, horizon=0.7, technology=0.7, quiet=0.4, routine=0.3. Figma matches almost exactly on participation (0.9) and technology (0.8). Key difference: repeatability at 0.1 vs attractor's 0.3 — Figma is more deeply embedded in daily routine than the attractor captures. The design tool is open all day, not just when creating something new. Also: nature_technology at 0.8 vs 0.7 — Figma is more purely digital than a typical creator-economy brand which may have physical outputs or community dimensions beyond the tool.

## Visual code
- **Typography:** Чистый гротеск — Inter или собственный шрифт. Функциональный, дружелюбный, никогда декоративный. Цветной текст как UI-элемент — пурпурные акцентные слова.
- **Palette:** Белый интерфейс + фирменный пурпурный (#7B68EE зона), серые UI-тона. Яркие цвета — только для метки компонентов и курсоров других пользователей. Каждый пользователь = свой цвет.
- **Geometry:** Сетка как принцип. Всё — рамки, компоненты, автолейауты. Скругления с математической точностью. Инструментальный интерфейс как визуальный стиль.
- **Density:** Средняя-высокая. Панели инструментов, слои, свойства — насыщенная информационная среда, но иерархически выстроенная.
- **Textures:** Экранный пиксель — единственная текстура. Плоский дизайн без теней и градиентов (в основном). Живые курсоры как социальная текстура.

## Verbal code
