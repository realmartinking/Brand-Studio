## Honda

Founded 1948 by Soichiro Honda in Hamamatsu, Japan. Started as a motorcycle manufacturer, expanded into automobiles (1963), power equipment, aviation, and robotics. Second-largest Japanese automaker by volume. Brand philosophy rooted in the founder's personal engineering passion — "The Power of Dreams" has been the global tagline since 2001. Wolff Olins contributed to European brand identity work. Honda's ASIMO robot and jet aircraft represent the brand's ambition to engineer across all domains of human movement.

## Why this position

Body-mind 0.6: Honda products are experienced through motion — riding, driving, operating. The brand foregrounds the physical experience of its machines but grounds it in engineering logic rather than pure sensation.

Serious-playful 0.4: The founder's engineering joy ("The Power of Dreams") gives Honda a genuine playfulness. Products like the Rube Goldberg "Cog" ad and the ASIMO robot embody curiosity-driven engineering rather than corporate seriousness.

Nature-technology 0.6: Technology brand at core — precision engineering, VTEC engines, hybrid systems. But Honda occupies a more human position than rivals: the technology serves dreams, not specs.

All-few 0.3: Mass-market positioning. From ¥100,000 Super Cub motorcycles (world's best-selling vehicle) to mainstream sedans. Democratic engineering.

Self-community 0.3: Individual ownership experience is primary. But Honda has strong enthusiast communities (NSX, Civic Type R, CB series) who bond around engineering appreciation.

## Attractor difference

Artisan-authentic fits Honda better than corporate-trust or bauhaus-functional: the brand carries genuine craft heritage — Soichiro Honda built engines by hand, the F1 program was engineer-led, not marketing-led. Key differences: Honda is more technology-coded (0.6 vs attractor's 0.3) because the engineering is the artisanship. Also more mass-market (all_few 0.3 vs 0.5) — artisan-authentic typically implies selective production, but Honda's craft scales. Adjacent to [[bauhaus-functional]] in form-follows-function engineering logic, and to [[silicon-optimism]] in its optimism about what engineering can achieve.
