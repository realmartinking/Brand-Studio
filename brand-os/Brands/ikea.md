## IKEA

The most visited furniture store in the world — 775 million customer visits per year. IKEA turned flat-pack furniture and forced store navigation into a global cultural institution. The meatball is as iconic as the bookshelf. The name is an acronym: Ingvar Kamprad Elmtaryd Agunnaryd.

## Why this position

All-few 0.1: The most democratic brand in this dataset. Every product is price-positioned at the median or below. The catalogue (until 2021, 200 million copies per year) was the most printed publication in the world after the Bible.

Community 0.6: Family is the central brand concept — the IKEA family, the home as shared space, the store as a family day out. Community over individual in every campaign.

Participation 0.7: Assembly is a brand feature, not a compromise — "the IKEA effect" (psychological investment from effort). The customer is a co-producer of their furniture.

Distance 0.2: First-name culture, the store restaurant, children's play areas, the staff in yellow t-shirts. Deliberately intimate for a brand of this scale.

Temperature 0.3: Warm — Swedish warmth, yellow and blue, wood tones, the canteen serving Swedish food. The store feels domestic even at 30,000 square metres.

Loud-quiet 0.3: IKEA is not a quiet brand — the catalogue was loud, the signage is loud, the product names are deliberate (and famously incomprehensible). But the visual system is restrained — clean photography, white space in catalogue layouts.

Control-release 0.7: The store maze (designed to force traversal of every section) is a supreme act of controlled customer experience. Nothing in an IKEA store is accidental.

Roots-horizon 0.4: Swedish design heritage is genuine (Scandinavian modernism is the product DNA) but IKEA also innovates — sustainability programs, flat-pack innovation, digital integration.

## Attractor difference

Nordic-purity attractor scores all_few=0.3, quiet=0.8, spacious=0.9. IKEA is dramatically more democratic (0.1 vs 0.3), louder (0.3 vs 0.8), and denser (0.4 vs 0.9). Nordic-purity is aspirational minimalism; IKEA is functional minimalism at mass scale. The store experience is deliberately dense and navigable — the opposite of spacious. IKEA is what nordic purity looks like when it has to serve 775 million customers.

## Visual code
- **Typography:** IKEA Sans — собственный шрифт, максимально нейтральный и читаемый на любом расстоянии. До 2009 — Futura. Демократичность как шрифтовой принцип.
- **Palette:** Синий IKEA (Pantone 294) + жёлтый (Pantone 116) = шведский флаг = демократия + солнце. Плюс белый и натуральное дерево.
- **Geometry:** Ровная сетка каталога. Комнаты-вселенные. Дотпланирование как визуальный язык. Название на этикетке — всегда в ВЕРХНЕМ РЕГИСТРЕ.
- **Density:** Средняя-высокая. Каталог насыщен сценами жизни. Ни одного пустого разворота.
- **Textures:** Birch veneer, white laminate, woven rattan, cotton percale, pine solid. Тактильные демократичные материалы.

## Verbal code
- **Tone:** Практически дружелюбный. Говорит как умный сосед, не как дизайнер. Никаких претензий на высокое.
- **Phrase length:** Средние. Конкретные задачи + решения. «Your home, your design, your price.»
- **Vocabulary:** Home, life, together, family, better, simple, organize, space, value, smart.
