## Juventus

Juventus Football Club was founded in 1897 in Turin, Italy, making it one of the oldest football clubs in the country. It is the most successful club in Italian football (Serie A record 36 Scudetti) and carries a global following across Europe, Asia, and South America. In January 2017, Juventus commissioned a radical rebrand that replaced the traditional oval badge (featuring a bull, stars, and shield) with a stark, minimalist "J" monogram — angular, black and white, fashion-forward. The rebrand, developed with Interbrand, was positioned as a transition from football club to global entertainment and lifestyle brand. The black-and-white vertical stripes ("bianconeri") are the most recognisable identity element and the foundation of the new mark.

## Why this position

**geometry 0.2:** The J logo is among the most angular, structurally honest marks in sport. Two parallel diagonal strokes forming a letterform, with no embellishment — a pure construction. The black-and-white stripes are the most structural pattern in football identity.

**contrast 0.1:** Maximum contrast — black on white, white on black. No gradients, no mid-tones in the primary brand system. The brand lives in absolute polarity.

**weight 0.1:** Visually, the brand is extremely light — thin strokes, sparse composition, maximum negative space. The J mark works at minimum line weight and feels almost typographic rather than emblematic.

**control_release 0.8:** The rebrand imposes a strict visual system — one palette, one mark, no heritage clutter. High control over brand expression. Licensing and fashion collaborations are carefully curated (Palace, adidas, Supreme-adjacent drops).

**roots_horizon 0.6:** Juventus balances 120+ years of football heritage (bianconeri identity, Champions League victories) with a deliberate horizon push into fashion and lifestyle. The rebrand explicitly chose future over tradition, unlike most football clubs.

**nature_technology 0.3:** The brand is structural and constructed — human-made, geometrically resolved. Neither natural nor technological; it is purely formal. Architecture and graphic design are better references than either pole.

## Visual code

- **Typography:** The Juventus wordmark uses a custom angular sans — condensed, geometric, uppercase. Secondary communications use clean grotesque. The system is typographically spare and high-contrast.
- **Palette:** White (#FFFFFF) and Black (#000000) — binary, absolute. Gold is used only for trophy/heritage contexts. The bianconeri stripe is the pattern system.
- **Geometry:** The J monogram: two diagonal strokes forming a letterform — sharp angles, no curves. The stripe pattern extends across merchandise, packaging, and digital surfaces. All layout is grid-strict.
- **Density:** Medium-high in merchandise and fashion contexts; sparse in brand/identity applications. The stripe fills space when needed.
- **Textures:** Technical fabric textures (adidas partnership — HEAT.RDY, AEROREADY) in sportswear; premium printed paper in brand communications. No warm textures — the brand is cool and hard.

- **Tone:** Proud, direct, fashion-aware. The brand speaks with the confidence of a club that does not need to explain its history — but also does not trade on it exclusively.
