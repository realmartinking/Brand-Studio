## LEGO

LEGO was founded in 1932 in Billund, Denmark by Ole Kirk Christiansen; the iconic interlocking ABS plastic brick was patented in its current form in 1958. The brand operates across toys, media, theme parks (LEGOLAND), and digital games, making it one of the world's most recognised play brands. Its audience spans children globally and a significant adult fan community (AFOLs). The 2012 turnaround under CEO Jørgen Vig Knudstorp repositioned LEGO from near-bankruptcy to the world's most profitable toy company, built on the tagline "Play On."

## Why this position

**serious_playful 0.9:** LEGO is the most play-forward brand at global scale. The entire product is a play proposition — building as imagination engine. AFOLs bring earnestness, but the underlying brand is relentlessly playful.

**all_few 0.2:** LEGO targets the broadest possible demographic — from 4-year-olds to 80-year-old architects. Mass global distribution, accessible price points, sold in every country. One of the least exclusive mass brands in existence.

**loud_quiet 0.1:** Primary red, yellow, blue, and green on white backgrounds. The LEGO logo in white on red is visible from 30 metres. No whispering here — visual presence is maximum.

**participation 0.8:** The product IS participation. You cannot passively consume LEGO — the object demands assembly. Community building events, conventions (Brickworld, BrickFair), shared MOC culture. Co-creation at scale.

**temperature 0.2:** Saturated warm primaries — red and yellow dominate the visual identity. The warmth is cheerful, not cosy; energetic, not intimate.

**density 0.2:** LEGO sets are individually dense (hundreds of pieces), but brand communication uses one product on white ground. Marketing is clean and product-first.

## Visual code

- **Typography:** Custom LEGO logotype — a rounded bold slab with tight tracking, white on red. Product packaging uses a proprietary display grotesque for age ranges and theme names; secondary text is a clean humanist sans. Type is always large, high-contrast, and legible from shelf distance.
- **Palette:** LEGO Red (#DA291C), LEGO Yellow (#FFD700), LEGO Blue (#006DB7), LEGO Green (#00833E), White (#FFFFFF). Brand system uses red as primary; product colours extend across the full spectrum by theme (Ninjago black/gold, Friends pastels, Technic dark grey/lime).
- **Geometry:** The brick stud grid is the foundational geometry — repeating circles on rectangles, strict orthogonal alignment. The logo has rounded corners that soften the rectangular frame.
- **Density:** Low in brand communications — hero product on white, no competing background elements. Sets themselves are high-density objects.
- **Textures:** Smooth ABS plastic — a specific tactile signature that is globally recognisable. Packaging is matte card with a gloss logo spot. No natural textures in brand materials.

## Verbal code

- **Tone:** Enthusiastic, inclusive, age-agnostic. Never talks down to children; never patronises adults. The brand believes in the builder.
- **Phrase length:** Short and action-forward. Imperative constructions preferred. "Build. Play. Imagine."
- **Vocabulary:** Building verbs (build, create, imagine, construct, explore), possibility nouns (world, story, adventure, idea), inclusive pronouns (everyone, you, together).
- **Examples:** "Play On." / "Only the best is good enough." / "Rebuild the world." / "Inspire and develop the builders of tomorrow."
