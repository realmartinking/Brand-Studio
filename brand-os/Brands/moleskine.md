## Moleskine

The brand claims the notebook of Chatwin, Hemingway, and Picasso — and the claim is partly mythological and entirely effective. The Italian company relaunched the "legendary" notebook format in 1997. The black hardcover, the elastic closure, the pocket in the back, the ribbon bookmark. A $20 notebook that sells on the proposition of who you might become while using it.

## Why this position

Self-community 0.3: Deeply individual — the notebook is private by definition. Writers, artists, and thinkers work alone. The brand does not celebrate the group using Moleskine; it celebrates the individual mind that the notebook serves.

Participation 0.8: The highest participation score among non-digital brands in this dataset. The notebook is entirely blank — the brand produces the container; the user produces all the content. Every Moleskine is finished by its owner.

Roots-horizon 0.4: The invented heritage (Chatwin never used a "Moleskine" specifically) sits at the roots end but the brand continuously extends into digital (Moleskine apps, digital paper) — roots-leaning but not rooted.

Nature-technology 0.3: Paper, ink, leather-like cover, elastic band. The product is analog in an increasingly digital world — this is the positioning, not just the material reality.

Body-mind 0.5: The act of writing is both physical (the pen on paper, the tactile resistance of the page) and mental (thinking through writing). The brand captures both: "The Legendary Notebooks" tagline is mind; the feel of the paper is body.

Contrast 0.3: High contrast — black cover, white pages. The notebook in a hand or on a table is a stark visual. The simplicity is the design.

Geometry 0.3: Angular — the rectangular notebook is a pure geometric object. No softening, no decoration, no curves.

Distance 0.3: Intimate — the notebook is personal property. The Moleskine voice addresses you as a creator, a thinker, a traveller. The brand respects your privacy; the product holds your secrets.

Temperature 0.4: Slightly warm — black cover, cream pages, the romantic heritage of traveller's notebooks, Paris cafes, literary culture. Not cold, but not warm either.

## Visual code
- **Typography:** Clarendon или фирменный slab serif — журналистская серьёзность. В digital — Georgia или Freight Text. Никаких гротесков.
- **Palette:** Чёрный переплёт как главная форма. Кремовый блок. Красная закладка. Эластик. Это и есть палитра.
- **Geometry:** Прямоугольник записной книжки как священная форма. Все производные — из этого прямоугольника.
- **Density:** Средняя. Архивные фотографии путешественников и писателей. Истории наполняют страницы как записи.
- **Textures:** Оилклот-переплёт (имитация клеёнки), кремовая бумага 70g/m², нитяное шитьё, карман для бумаг. Материал как история.
