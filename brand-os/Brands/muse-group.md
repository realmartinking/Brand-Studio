## Muse Group

Muse Group is a technology company that serves as the parent holding company for a portfolio of musician tools, including MuseScore (free music notation software, 20M+ registered users), Ultimate Guitar (the world's largest guitar tabs platform with 600M+ tab downloads), and Audacity (open-source audio editor, one of the most downloaded free software applications historically). The company was founded in 2020 and acquired these tools to build an integrated ecosystem for musicians at all skill levels. COLLINS worked on Muse Group's brand positioning and identity to articulate the holding company's unifying mission: giving musicians the tools to express themselves.

## Why this position

- **all_few: 0.2** — MuseScore is free. Audacity is free and open-source. Ultimate Guitar has a massive freemium base. Muse Group's portfolio is deliberately democratically priced — the musician audience spans beginner to professional, economy to business.
- **nature_technology: 0.7** — All three products are software tools. The Muse Group brand story is about technology enabling music creation — notation software replacing manuscript paper, digital audio editing replacing tape, tab notation replacing printed books.
- **creator-economy alignment** — The portfolio defines the musician creator economy: MuseScore for notation and sharing scores, Ultimate Guitar for learning songs, Audacity for recording and editing. Muse Group provides the full creator stack for musicians.
- **distance: 0.3** — "By musicians for musicians" is the genuine positioning. The company acquired tools that musicians already loved and used — the relationship begins from peer-level trust.
- **participation: 0.3** — MuseScore's score-sharing community (5M+ scores), Ultimate Guitar's tab community (millions of user-submitted tabs). Community content IS the product.

## Attractor difference

[[creator-economy]] is the natural fit: tools for creators (musicians), community participation, enabling user output, peer-level brand voice. Muse Group is to musicians what Figma is to designers — an enabling platform. Key difference: Muse Group operates across a more diverse audience (beginner guitarists through professional composers) than typical creator-economy brands, and its products have a longer heritage (Audacity 2000, MuseScore 2002) than digital-native creator tools, giving it a slightly stronger roots signal (roots_horizon: 0.6).

Adjacent attractors that nuance Muse Group's position:

- [[intellectual-craft]] — professional composers using MuseScore represent intellectual-craft users; the depth of notation software's feature set for serious musicians
