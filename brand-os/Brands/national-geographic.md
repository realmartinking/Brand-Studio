## National Geographic

National Geographic Society founded 1888 in Washington D.C. The magazine launched 1888, the iconic yellow border introduced 1910. Chermayeff & Geismar refined the identity system. 135+ years of exploration, science, and photography. Now owned by The Walt Disney Company (majority stake, 2019). The yellow rectangle — used to frame the cover and as a standalone mark — is one of the most recognized brand elements in publishing history. "Further" is the brand positioning — an invitation to go deeper.

## Why this position

Geometry 0.8: The yellow rectangle border is pure geometry — a frame, a window, a threshold. Maximally simple: a rectangle in yellow. Its power comes entirely from consistency and scale of use over 100+ years.

Contrast 0.8: Yellow (#FFCC00) against black or photographic backgrounds creates maximum visual contrast. The yellow border works as a signal system — it means "important, worth looking at."

Nature-technology 0.3: Firmly nature-oriented. The content is overwhelmingly natural world — wildlife, geography, indigenous cultures, ocean, space. Technology appears as tool for exploration, not the subject.

Distance 0.6: The brand speaks from a position of authority — researchers, scientists, explorers. The editorial voice is expert, not conversational. The reader follows the expert.

Serious-playful 0.4: The subject matter is serious — conservation, science, climate — but the photography creates wonder and delight. Not playful but not grim.

## Attractor difference

Wild-adventure is the nearest attractor: nature immersion, exploration, physical world, authoritative guidance. Key differences: National Geographic has significantly more institutional authority ([[cultural-institution]] adjacency) — 135-year scientific society, Pulitzer Prize-winning photography, peer-reviewed content standards. Also more media/publication-coded than a pure outdoor brand. The yellow border as geometry (0.8) is much more distinctive than wild-adventure's typical visual language. Adjacent to [[earth-mother]] for environmental advocacy and [[cultural-institution]] for the scientific and educational mission.

## Verbal code

- **Tone:** Authoritative and wondering simultaneously. The expert who never loses their sense of awe.
- **Phrase length:** Varies — headlines are punchy, body text is long-form journalism. The brand positioning is short: "Further."
- **Vocabulary:** Explore, discover, further, science, wild, earth, ocean, species, expedition, wonder, planet.
- **Examples:** "Further." / "Inspiring people to care about the planet since 1888." / "See More. Feel More."
