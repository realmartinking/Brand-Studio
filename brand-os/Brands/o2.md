## O2

UK mobile network operator, originally spun off from British Telecom in 2001 as mmO2, rebranded to O2 in 2002 by Wolff Olins. Now a subsidiary of Telefónica, operating primarily in the UK and Germany. The Wolff Olins identity uses the "O2" wordmark set in a bold sans-serif, surrounded by floating blue bubbles of varying sizes on a gradient blue background — an immediately recognisable visual language that has remained consistent for over two decades. The bubble aesthetic codes as approachable, digital, slightly playful. O2 is notable for building its brand around community and priority rewards (O2 Priority), positioning the network as a benefit platform rather than just connectivity infrastructure.

## Why this position

Self-community 0.6: O2 built a brand around community — "Be More Dog" campaigns, O2 Priority (rewards for loyal customers), O2 Arena naming rights. The network is positioned as a membership with benefits, not just a utility.

Participation 0.6: O2 Priority invites active participation — discover deals, unlock experiences, redeem rewards. The brand asks you to do things.

Distance 0.2: O2's voice is unusually warm and informal for a telecoms brand. "Be More Dog" was a celebrated campaign for embracing life with enthusiasm. Close, casual, energetic.

Roots-horizon 0.7: Launched 2002, relatively young. Forward-looking in positioning — connectivity as gateway to future experiences.

All-few 0.3: Mass consumer network serving tens of millions of UK subscribers. Broad demographic targeting.

Temperature 0.4: The blue bubble palette reads as cool-digital, but the brand voice is warm and humanistic. Cool visual code with warm verbal code — intentional tension.

Rhythm 0.4: Mobile connectivity is always-on background rhythm. The brand uses events (Priority experiences) to create peaks in a flat usage pattern.

- **Geometry:** Circles — the bubble system is the primary geometric language. Circles of different sizes, floating, overlapping, arranged in clusters. The "O" of O2 echoes the bubble form. No angular elements in the primary system.

## Verbal code

- **Tone:** Warm, energetic, occasionally playful. The "Be More Dog" campaign set a tone of life-embracing enthusiasm unusual in telecoms. Not corporate — conversational.
- **Phrase length:** Short to medium. Campaign-style punchy lines. App and service copy is clear and direct.
- **Vocabulary:** Life and experience language (discover, unlock, enjoy, experience), network as enabler (stay connected, wherever you go), community signals (Priority, members, together), enthusiasm markers (more, better, now).
- **Examples:** "Be More Dog." / "Priority. Because you're worth it." / "The network made for you."
