## Robinhood

Robinhood Markets is a US financial services company founded in 2013 by Vlad Tenev and Baiju Bhatt, headquartered in Menlo Park, California. The platform pioneered commission-free stock trading, disrupting established brokerages (Fidelity, Schwab) and forcing the industry to eliminate trading fees. COLLINS redesigned Robinhood's brand identity, creating the feather-and-wordmark logo system and the visual language supporting the "Investing for Everyone" mission. The 2021 GameStop episode made Robinhood a flashpoint for retail investor democratization debates.

## Why this position

- **all_few: 0.2** — Zero commissions, no account minimums, mobile-first design. Robinhood's founding thesis is financial democratization — the brand is explicitly designed to reach everyone who was previously excluded from investing.
- **roots_horizon: 0.9** — Founded 2013, completely forward-looking. No heritage, no history to protect. The brand narrative is disruption: "why should Wall Street be the only ones to profit?"
- **nature_technology: 0.8** — Mobile-first product, algorithmic order routing, technology enables the business model (payment for order flow). The tech is what makes zero commissions possible.
- **distance: 0.2** — "Investing for Everyone" collapses the traditional distance of financial institutions. Robinhood uses first-person casual language, emoji-like iconography, confetti on first trade.
- **speed: 0.2** — Account opening in minutes. Trades execute instantly. Real-time market data. The product competes on speed versus traditional brokerages.

## Verbal code

- **Tone:** Accessible, optimistic, slightly rebellious. The brand frames traditional finance as the enemy and itself as the liberator.
- **Phrase length:** Short. Punchy. Anti-jargon. The brand avoids financial terminology that would exclude new investors.
