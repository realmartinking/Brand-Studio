## Slack

Workplace communication platform launched 2013 by Stewart Butterfield (Flickr co-founder), headquartered in San Francisco. Acquired by Salesforce in 2021 for $27.7 billion. The 2019 Pentagram rebrand — led by Michael Bierut — replaced the original octothorpe logo (a hashtag made of speech bubbles) with a simplified version: four rotated pairs of overlapping speech-bubble shapes forming a pinwheel, in four colours (purple, green, yellow, cyan). The palette shifted from an overly vibrant multi-colour system to a more controlled four-colour identity. The rebrand addressed brand dilution: the original logo failed to reproduce consistently across contexts.

## Why this position

Self-community 0.7: Slack is a team tool — channels, threads, shared context. The product is meaningless without community. Individual accounts serve collective work.

Control-release 0.5: The product enables both structured (channels, org hierarchy) and unstructured (DMs, emoji reactions, casual tone) communication. Balance is the product philosophy.

Roots-horizon 0.8: Launched 2013, already feels institutional in tech timescales. But the brand presents as forward-looking — "Where work happens" as an open-ended horizon statement.

Body-mind 0.8: Slack is built on immediacy of response — the notification ping, the typing indicator, the emoji reaction. It engages reflex as much as thought.

Participation 0.7: The product architecture rewards active participation — reactions, threads, mentions. Lurking is possible but counter to the design intent.

Distance 0.2: The brand voice is collegial, first-name, informal. "Hey" not "Dear." The closest enterprise software gets to casual.

Rhythm 0.3: Async but always-on. Messages arrive at unpredictable intervals. The rhythm is continuous low-level hum, not structured beats.

Serious-playful 0.6: Enterprise software that allows custom emoji, Slack-bot jokes, and has an official "Tone of Voice" that explicitly uses humor. The playfulness is calibrated — not Duolingo, not IBM.

## Visual code

- **Typography:** Slack Sans — custom typeface based on Circular. Rounded geometric sans, warm and approachable. Medium weight in headlines, regular in body. All-lowercase brand voice reinforces proximity.
  *Source: training-data / Pentagram 2019 case*
- **Palette:** Aubergine (#4A154B) as the primary brand color — deep purple, unusual in tech. Supported by Slack Yellow (#ECB22E), Slack Green (#2EB67D), Slack Cyan (#36C5F0), Slack Red (#E01E5A). Four-color logo on white or aubergine background.
  *Source: training-data*
- **Geometry:** The pinwheel logo — four pairs of speech bubbles at 45° rotations, each pair a different color. Rounded shapes throughout. Buttons, cards, and UI elements use generous border-radius.
  *Source: training-data / Pentagram case*
- **Density:** Product UI is information-dense (message threads, channels list, emoji reactions). Marketing materials are lower density — generous whitespace, product screenshots as hero.
  *Source: training-data*
- **Textures:** Flat digital. Occasional illustration in marketing (loose, informal line illustrations). No texture, no material reference.
  *Source: training-data*

## Verbal code

- **Tone:** Collegial, clear, occasionally witty. The brand speaks the way a sharp colleague writes in Slack. Informal without being sloppy.
- **Phrase length:** Short to medium. "Where work happens." Subject + verb + location. Functional.
- **Vocabulary:** Work vocabulary made warm (team, together, channel, connect, huddle), second-person address ("your team"), contractions always, emoji permitted in brand copy.
- **Examples:** "Where work happens." / "Make work life simpler, more pleasant and more productive." / "Slack your team."
