## Supreme

A skateboard shop opened on Lafayette Street in 1994. Thirty years later, a box logo t-shirt resells for ten times retail. Supreme invented the weekly drop model, the collaboration as cultural event, and the queue as brand experience. It remains privately controlled and never fully explained.

## Why this position

Self-community 0.3: Supreme is individual (you got it, they didn't) but depends entirely on a community to generate value. The insider group is small by design — but the aspiring community is enormous.

Control-release 0.8: The brand appears chaotic — random collaborations, limited drops — but every release is calculated. The scarcity is engineered. The chaos is managed.

Loud-quiet 0.1: The box logo is one of the loudest marks in fashion — red rectangle, Futura Heavy Oblique, white. On a white t-shirt. No subtlety. The message is that you don't need subtlety when you have the mark.

Geometry 0.2 / Contrast 0.1: Angular red/white, maximum contrast. The visual system hasn't changed since Barbara Kruger.

Rhythm 0.1 / Speed 0.1: Thursday drops. 11am. Fast as possible — products sell out in seconds. The fastest consumer experience in fashion.

Distance 0.2: The store staff are community members. The culture is insider and intimate — if you know the store, you know the people.

Repeatability 0.8: Each drop is an event. The routine is the weekly event cycle — paradox of routine-as-event is Supreme's structural achievement.

Participation 0.4: The community generates significant culture around the brand — resale markets, unboxing videos, authentication communities. Not co-creation in the [[figma|Figma]] sense, but active cultural participation.

## Attractor difference

Urban-energy attractor is the closest fit — drop culture, loud, fast, angular, community-coded. Supreme differs in one important dimension: the brand's cultural position is more locked to a specific heritage (NYC skate, 1990s downtown) than urban-energy's more general street-energy framing. Roots-horizon 0.5 vs attractor's 0.6 reflects this — Supreme is genuinely rooted, not just street-coded. Also: all_few is closer to 0.5 than 0.4 — Supreme wants the aspiration of exclusivity but distributes at scale through global stores.

## Visual code
- **Typography:** Futura Heavy Oblique — красный блок, белые буквы. Это ВСЁ. Нет второй типографической системы. Логотип IS шрифт IS бренд.
- **Palette:** Красный Supreme (Pantone 485) + белый + чёрный. Иногда бокс-лого адаптируется в коллаборациях под партнёра.
- **Geometry:** Квадрат. Один квадрат. Box Logo — священная геометрия. Всё остальное — вариации на тему.
- **Density:** Экстремально низкая или экстремально высокая. Каталожные съёмки — один скейтер. Коллаборации — медиа-взрыв.
- **Textures:** Деним, cotton fleece, нейлон, skateboard grip tape, skateboard deck. Всё из уличного.
