## Target up&up

Target up&up is Target Corporation's private label brand covering everyday household essentials — cleaning products, personal care, paper goods, vitamins, and OTC medications. Launched in 2009 as a rebranding of Target's previous private label lines, up&up positions itself as a quality alternative to national brands at a lower price point, typically 20-30% less than comparable branded products. COLLINS redesigned the up&up visual identity to elevate the private label beyond generic store-brand aesthetic — creating a cohesive design system with a distinctive upward-arrows symbol, clean typography, and color-coded category system that helps shoppers navigate the 700+ product range.

## Why this position

- **all_few: 0.1** — Up&up covers everything from vitamins to cleaning supplies to baby products. The brand serves every household need at economy pricing. Maximum breadth, maximum accessibility.
- **repeatability: 0.1** — Paper towels, laundry detergent, shampoo, ibuprofen — these are weekly or bi-weekly purchases. No brand in the database has higher purchase frequency combined with passive buying decision.
- **distance: 0.2** — Private label is the most intimate brand relationship in retail. It is inside your home, in your medicine cabinet, under your sink. The brand is physically present in daily life in a way that no aspirational brand achieves.
- **geometry: 0.6** — COLLINS's up&up design system uses the distinctive double-arrow upward symbol, rounded forms, and clean category color-coding. Friendly geometry that differentiates from generic drugstore private label aesthetics.
- **serious_playful: 0.5** — Target's brand DNA adds playfulness and design consideration to functional household products. Up&up is not grim economy — it is cheerful value.
