## Tesla

The only car brand in history that achieved desirability without advertising. Tesla sells the idea that you can save the planet and go 0–100 in under 3 seconds simultaneously. The Model 3 made electric cars aspirational before they were practical.

## Why this position

Roots-horizon 0.9: Maximum future orientation. No heritage — this is the point. Tesla exists because the existing automotive industry couldn't imagine this product. Every communication is about what comes next.

Nature-technology 0.9: Entirely technological — the car is a software product on wheels. OTA updates, autopilot, battery management. But the mission is nature-aligned (clean energy). The tension is productive.

All-few 0.5: Positioned between accessible and exclusive — the Model 3 is mass-market aspirational, the Model S and X are premium, the Cybertruck is subcultural. The brand covers more of the spectrum than clean-tech typically does.

Control-release 0.7: Software-style releases — features appear overnight, build quality has been variable, Elon Musk tweets about product specs before launch. The control is in the technology stack, not in the traditional brand management sense.

Loud-quiet 0.5: The brand itself is visual quiet (no advertising, clean stores) but the cultural noise around it is extreme — the CEO is the loudest brand signal. Unusual split: product aesthetics are quiet, brand conversation is loud.

Speed 0.5: Delivery events are ceremonial (the keys, the first drive); day-to-day OTA updates are instant. Both rhythms coexist.

Density 0.8: Stores and marketing use extreme whitespace — the car is presented in a void, nothing competes.

Repeatability 0.6: A car purchase is an event, but Tesla owners update software routinely — the product refreshes without the purchase event repeating.

## Attractor difference

Clean-tech attractor: all_few=0.5, nature_technology=0.8, roots_horizon=0.7. Tesla pushes further into the future (0.9 vs 0.7) and is more technologically coded (0.9 vs 0.8). The attractor doesn't account for Tesla's unusual CEO-as-brand dynamic which injects loudness into an otherwise quiet aesthetic brand. Also: Tesla's repeatability score (0.6) is higher than clean-tech attractor's (0.4) because of the software update rhythm that makes ownership feel ongoing.

## Visual code
- **Typography:** Tesla Type — собственный гротеск, чёткий и технологичный. Тонкие начертания для корпоративного использования, Bold для продуктов.
- **Palette:** Чёрный + белый + красный акцент. Серебро металлика — экстерьер. Никаких корпоративных сине-красных. Технологическая нейтральность.
- **Geometry:** Обтекаемость — Cybertruck сломал это правило намеренно. Угловатость как провокация внутри обтекаемого бренда.
- **Density:** Низкая. Продукт + горизонт. Ничего кроме машины.
- **Textures:** Матовое стекло, веган-кожа, голый металл, LED-подсветка. Отсутствие органических текстур принципиально.
