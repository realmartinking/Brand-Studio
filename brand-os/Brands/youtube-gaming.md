## YouTube Gaming

YouTube Gaming is the dedicated gaming vertical of YouTube, launched in 2015 to compete with Twitch's dominance in live game streaming. The platform serves both VOD gaming content (Let's Plays, reviews, walkthroughs) and live streaming, covering competitive esports, casual streaming, and gaming culture commentary. COLLINS worked on YouTube Gaming's brand identity, creating a visual system that extends YouTube's core red identity into gaming-specific contexts — darker, more energetic, and neon-accented to match gaming aesthetic expectations. The platform reaches hundreds of millions of gaming-content viewers monthly through YouTube's infrastructure.

## Why this position

- **participation: 0.1** — Live streaming is participation as the core mechanic. Chat, SuperChats (paid messages), polls, channel points, raids. The audience is not passive — viewer participation is the entertainment. This is maximum participation in the entire brand space.
- **rhythm: 0.1** — Live streaming = no pause, no editing, real-time. Clips go viral instantly. Streaming schedules create daily rituals. Gaming content cycle (launches, tournaments) drives content calendar.
- **serious_playful: 0.8** — Gaming is fundamentally play. Even competitive esports frames itself as entertainment. The gaming content aesthetic — screaming commentary, meme formats, "poggers" culture — is deep in playful territory.
- **contrast: 0.1** — Gaming visual language is extreme contrast: dark backgrounds, neon highlights (RGB peripherals, streamer lighting), high-saturation accent colors. YouTube Gaming's visual identity follows this convention.
- **distance: 0.1** — The parasocial intimacy of streaming collapses creator-audience distance completely. Streamers know subscribers' names. Chat creates direct two-way communication illusion. Extremely close relationship.
